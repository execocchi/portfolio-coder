UX Process

Research

Let´s start from the very beginning...

We stated by interviewing our potential users and having focus groups to try and understand their needs. Since the app was new, we had really big horizons and we saved every idea that came up for the future.

Interviews

From a mentor perspective , we wanted to know how much real time they could really devote to a child in their daily basis. As we knew from the beginning, mentor will be busy people and we wanted them to have an engagement with FIVE but at the same time not to feel it as an obligation. On the other hand, we also had quick talks with high school students to understand what they will like and what will help them have engagement with an app that will need their compromise on completing tasks and reading interesting stuff.

Focus Groups

We divided children between 12 and 16 years old into groups around 6 kids. This helped us understand the needs of the kids in a safe environment, where they could discuss and share their thoughts with others. We used moderating techniques to help the kids feel comfortable and interact to start to know each other. We took a lot of feedback from these potential users, it helped us as lot to empae with them, as they achieved to show themselves in a really good atmosphere with no judgement.

User Flow and Interactions

With all the information from our first research stage, so we had our starting point...

Application Process and First Time App Use

We need to give a very good first impression and a nice user experience, so we took a deep thought on how to work on these first experiences for both our mentors and mentees. We created users tasks flows that reflected all the steps an alternatives before we started wireframing the whole app flow.

Wire frames and Flows

Once we had a deeper idea on how the main flows would be, we started wireframing and thinking the all the first interactions.

Interaction Patterns

“Experience is critical, for it determines how fondly people remember their interactions.-” - Don Norman in The Design of Everyday Things

Feedback

Every action needs a reaction. We used a short animation to show when an emoji is selected.

Visibility

Users need to know what the options are In every place where the user can do something, they have button for the main actions

Affordance

Users need to understand something by what it looks like The camera icon is a known pattern for opening the camera

Consistency

The same action has to cause the same reaction, every time All cards work the same way in all the app

Constraints

Limiting the range of interaction We don’t want to confuse the user, and we just show possible action
