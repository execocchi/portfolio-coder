Clarín

Clarín is an Argentinian morning newspaper published in the city of Buenos Aires which was founded on 28 August, 1945. It’s a digital version, Clarin.com, is the third most followed spanish digital newspaper in the world with 8.179.000 of unique users.

The Solution

Following the world’s main news media international trends, such as New York Times or The Guardian, Clarín has created a paywall to five access to it’s digital product despite the fact the daily consumption is in decline.

Challenge

We need to persuade the users so as to convince them that this was necessary to pay for something that until then had been free online. The printed newspaper had to be paid but not the digital version.

Research

We need to understand our users and what they are expecting

As the newspaper already has a lot of unique users, we wanted to understand what they valued the most and why they have chosen it to inform themselves each day.

User Testing

We tested the product with 10 users, 5 heavy users (those who express total loyalty to the newspaper) and 5 swallows (those who read a bit of different types of news media without expressing loyalty)

We decided to test in these two groups to understand what made them choose this newspaper and also what they valued, if someone is expressing total loyalty we don’t want to lose them, but at the same time, we want the users that compare different media and see what their expectations are.

Focus Groups

We had 3 focus groups, two with heavy users and one mixed, between heavy and swallows. We didn’t tell the users we were working with Clarin, the main idea of these was to take real insights from users, and work on a safe environment. We used several moderating techniques, like a ball that they could be passing to each other so everyone got it’s chance to speak. In these focus groups is really normal for the users to want to share their personal opinion so it’s important to not lose the focus and keep it neutral.

How to use the insights

After we gathered all the insights and understood the reasons why users where choosing Clarín we decided to reinforce all the positive aspects, and try to improve the things that were confusing for the users. We wanted them to have a experience inside the newspaper, both in mobile and desktop. Regarding the paywall, what we needed to do was to show the users the value of the product and by this convince them that what they will be paying for was good and they will be happy with it. We also propose, as an improvement, giving more benefits to the payed users, such as discounts on other products or extra functionalities such as personal recommendations.
